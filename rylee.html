Cat Facts

The cat also known as a domestic cat and house cat, is a small carnivorous mammal. It requires a predominantly meat based diet. They has retractable claws that are adaptable to killing small prey species such as mice and rats. Cats are strong, have quick reflexes and a powerful bite. Although loving when tamed, these adorable creatures are fierce and won't hesitate to bite you for absolutely no reason.

Fact 1:

Cats can make over 100 different sounds. Hissing, growling and meowing are some of the more commonly known sounds cats make. However cats have a very large range of vocalizations. Like purring for example.

Fact 2:

Have you ever wondered why cats purr? It's because of multiple different factors! The most commonly known reason is cats purr when they are content, however cats have been known to purr when they are anxious or sick. It is also a way to show they want attention or if they are just greeting you!

Fact 3:

Cats do not hate being wet. If a cat is submerged in water it triggers a fear response as most cats can't swim. Its instinctive.

Quick Facts Information
Purring in big cats Big cats like Tigers, Lions, Jaugars, leopards and many more happen to purr as well. Fun fact a lions roar is closely associated with purring.
Most cats are lactose intolerant Despite popular belief, most cats are actually lactose intolerant. Giving cats milk is not good for them.
Whiskers! All cats have a total of 24 whiskers, 12 on each side. Cats whiskers connect to a part of their brain that is similar to the visual cortex on humans. They can see using their whiskers!
Catnaps! Cats sleep for 2/3 of their lives. Sleep is a key factor in kitten development.
History of Egypt and cats If a cat is killed in Egypt the punishment is automatically the death penalty. Mau is the name of the oldest cat breed in the world.
